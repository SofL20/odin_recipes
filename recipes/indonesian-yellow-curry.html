Indonesian Yellow Curry

Description

The paste for this curry gives it a lovely sunny colour and an incredibly zingy flavour. The courgettes give body and the peppers provide bite, while the coconut and lemongrass rice compliments the curry perfectly. All in all, this is a really well-rounded, satisfying meal that's perfect for any night of the week.

Ingredients

For the curry

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 large courgette
  • 1 red pepper
  • 2 limes
  • Black pepper

For the spice paste

    • 1 lemongrass stalk
    • Thumb-sized piece of fresh ginger
    • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
    • 1 tbsp ground turmeric
    • 1 green chilli
    • 2 tbsp coconut oil
    • 2 tsp soy sauce
    • Splash of water

For the rice

    • 100g basmati rice
    • 200 ml water
    • Generous pinch of salt
    • 1x 400g tin coconut milk
    • 1 lemongrass stalk

To serve

    • Handful of fresh coriander
    • Handful of toasted coconut flakes

Steps

  1. Make the spice paste

    • Put all the spice paste ingredients into a small food processor or blender and blitz to a coarse paste
    • Careful with the turmeric as it will stain!

  2. Make the rice

    • Put the rice, water and salt into a small lidded saucepan
    • Carefully open the tin of coconut milk without shaking it, scooping out the think coconut cream from the top and set it aside, then pour the milk into the pan
    • Bash the lemongrass stalk with a rolling pin or the back of a knife and add it into the pan
    • Cover and bring to the boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes

  3. Meanwhile, make the curry

    • Set the frying pan over a medium heat
    • Melt the coconut oil
    • Add the curry paste and cook until it starts to sizzle
    • Chop the courgette at an angle into long discs and add to the pan, stirring to coat with the paste
    • Fry for about 5 minutes until starting to turn golden
    • Roughly chop the red pepper and add it to the pan
    • Add the reserved coconut cream and stir through, then leave to cook for 2-3 minutes, until the vegetables are just tender
    • Zest one of the limes into the pan, then halve it and squeeze in the juice
    • Cut the remaining lime into 4 wedges and set aside
    • Season to taste with black pepper

  4. To finish

    • Holding a sieve over the curry pan, drain the cooked rice over the top of the curry, letting the starchy cooking water enrich the sauce
    • Discard the lemongrass
    • Place the rice in the sieve back on top of the rice pan and cover with a tea towel
    • Put the small frying pan over a medium heat
    • Sprinkle over the coconut flakes and toast until starting to colour
    • Take off the heat

  5. Serve

    • Spoon the rice and curry into bowls and top with coriander and coconut flakes
    • Serve with the lime wedges
